The one thing to fix first

The Vehicle Detail Page — the page whose only job is to produce a lead — currently makes buyers log in to “Unlock Contact Details” and offers a “Make an Offer” bidding form. The spec is explicit: no page requires login, and there is no auction/bidding.

So the primary conversion surface is actively suppressing the single metric the whole product exists for. Everything else in this report is secondary to unwinding that.

Part B★

Inspiration from Cars24

merchandising · trust · SEO

Reviewed the live cars24.com.au home + listing pages. Cars24 is a full transactional retailer — buy online, home delivery, 30-day returns, pay upfront. That machinery is exactly what this product excludes (and why no payment processor is needed). What’s worth borrowing is how they merchandise trust and win search.

MustDiscount & “price drop” chips

Was→now price + savings chip on cards. Schema already has previous_price — needs the card UI. Honest urgency.

MustTrust proof grid

A scannable icon grid of your proof points — inspection, roadworthy, warranty, Google rating — on home + VDP.

MustCondition report + defect photos

First-class VDP condition block, honest defect close-ups, and an “imperfections & safety” filter. The anti-marketplace edge.

MustEditorial listing SEO

Live-count H1, intro copy, “popular models” internal-link table, FAQ on every landing page. They do this textbook-well.

MustTestimonials with source badges

Named stories, “via ProductReview”, star + aggregate rating — surfaced near CTAs, not just on /testimonials.

MustCity hubs both ways

/used-cars-in-{city} and /sell-my-car-in-{city} cross-linked in a footer link lattice.

ShouldIn-grid promo interleaves

1–2 cross-sell cards (finance pre-approval, “got a car to sell?”) woven into the inventory grid.

ShouldCurated collection pages

“Family SUVs”, “First cars”, “Tradie utes”, “Luxury” — long-tail SEO + human browse beyond make/model.

ShouldQuality-tier badge

Optional “Premium Selection” tier per vehicle as a merchandising signal. Not in the schema yet.

Could · V2Instant valuation

Rules-based trade-in range. V1 keeps the sell form; note as a future upsell.

Do not copy — the transactional layer

Buy-online checkout · pay-upfront · home delivery & doorstep logistics · 30-Day Return Guarantee · on-site finance lending · app-download push.

Copying any of these would need the payment processor you explicitly don’t want, and would dilute the site’s single job: capture the lead, close it offline with a human. The winning position is the anti-marketplace one — every car is yours, inspected by you, backed by someone who answers in 15 minutes.

Part C

Order of operations

de-risk conversion first

Fix the lead path, then shed weight, then fill gaps. Lint · typecheck · test after each step — ESLint warnings fail CI.

  1. ● Fix VDP CTAs & remove unlock / bidding / messaging

    One coherent change — restores the core lead flow. Highest ROI.

  2. ● Remove buyer auth + account subsystem

    Plus Pro-plan API gating and the quick-lead route. Simplifies middleware, header, layout.

  3. ● Delete dead code, cruft & duplicate pages

    Fast, low-risk; shrinks the surface before deeper work.

  4. ● Strip stale server/schema logic — or run the fresh-Supabase reset

    The project reset the plan calls for subsumes most schema debt in one move.

  5. ● Build the missing must-haves

    Budget landing pages, finance calculator, full lead pipeline, lifecycle automation.

  6. ● Complete admin & SEO infrastructure

    Featured/merchandising, SEO manager, reports; JSON-LD + sitemap + redirects.

  7. ● Hardening & tests

    MFA, rate-limit tiers, anti-spam module, re-scoped test suite.

Critical — blocks the core product Major — debt / missing must-have Hygiene — cleanup Accent — the product’s own brand blue
