# Phase 7: Competitive SEO Benchmarking Plan

**Audit Context**: The Australian used vehicle market is heavily dominated by major players (Carsales, CarsGuide). We must benchmark against them to find our niche and out-execute them on modern SEO vectors (Core Web Vitals, GEO).

## Competitors to Benchmark
1. **Carsales.com.au** (The incumbent giant)
2. **CarsGuide.com.au** (Strong editorial/content SEO)
3. **Drive.com.au** (News and reviews heavy)

## What They Do Better
- **Massive Editorial Hubs**: They have thousands of test drives, comparisons, and "Best of" articles linking directly to inventory.
- **Extreme PSEO Granularity**: They have routes all the way down to `/[make]/[model]/[badge]/[year]/[city]/[suburb]`.
- **Domain Authority**: Decades of established backlinks.

## What This Project Can Copy Ethically
- **Deep Programmatic Hubs**: Replicate the Body Type + City and Budget + City architecture.
- **Review Snippets**: Display aggregated review scores on listing pages.

## What This Project Can Do Differently (Our Wedge)
- **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)**: Incumbents have massive, messy, ad-riddled DOMs that AI bots struggle to parse. Our extremely clean, semantic `<table>` facts and `llms.txt` approach will index much cleaner in SearchGPT and Google AI Overviews.
- **Core Web Vitals**: Incumbents suffer from heavy ad-tech bloat (poor CLS and LCP). We can maintain perfect 100/100 Lighthouse scores, winning the mobile-first indexing tie-breakers.
- **Direct Contact / Zero Fees**: Emphasize "No booking fees" and "Direct contact with vendors" in our Title and Meta Descriptions to drastically improve organic CTR over incumbents who hide seller details.

## Ranking Opportunity Gaps
- Long-tail localized queries (e.g., "cheap used vans in parramatta") are often neglected by the massive state-level hubs of competitors. Local Business schema on vendor profiles + hyper-local PSEO hubs can capture this.
